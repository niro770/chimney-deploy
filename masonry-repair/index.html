Frequently Asked Questions

Tuckpointing is the process of removing deteriorated mortar from the joints between bricks and replacing it with fresh mortar. It restores the waterproof seal and structural integrity.

Look for crumbling or missing mortar, staining on brick faces, cracks in the crown, bricks that are spalling or falling off, and water inside the firebox after rain.

Mortar joints typically last 25–30 years before needing tuckpointing. However, early repair when cracks first appear prevents much larger repair bills.

Tuckpointing runs $500–$1,500 for a typical chimney. Crown replacement: $300–$800. Full rebuilds: $3,000–$10,000+ depending on height and scope.
