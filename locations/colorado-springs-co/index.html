Why Colorado Springs residents choose ChimneyServices.net

Colorado Springs sits at 6,035 feet — nearly 800 feet higher than Denver — and the Pikes Peak region's chimney environment is distinctly more demanding than the Mile High City. Higher elevation means lower air pressure, affecting chimney draft even more significantly than Denver. The Front Range's temperature swings are extreme here: 70°F January days followed by -10°F nights within 48 hours are not uncommon. Colorado Springs averages 38 inches of annual snow — more than Denver — and the Palmer Divide's geography channels moisture-laden storms from the south that deposit heavy wet snow on chimney crowns. The city's strong military community (Fort Carson, Peterson AFB, Schriever AFB) creates a transient housing market where chimney maintenance histories are frequently unknown.

Colorado Springs' older Broadmoor, Ivywild and Old Colorado City neighborhoods have homes from the 1920s–1950s with original masonry chimneys that have survived the altitude and climate through quality construction. Newer developments in Briargate, Black Forest and Monument at elevation 7,000–8,000+ feet face even more extreme altitude-related draft challenges. Colorado's strong wood stove culture at altitude requires specific liner sizing that accounts for both the appliance output and the elevation adjustment — a calculation that general handymen often get wrong.

When to book: Colorado Springs' chimney season runs October through April. Book in August — earlier than Denver due to the higher elevation's earlier onset of cold. Altitude-related draft issues are best assessed during cool weather when the full seasonal draft pattern is active.
