Why Detroit residents choose ChimneyServices.net

Detroit's housing stock is among the oldest and most chimney-rich in the Midwest. The city's peak construction era — the 1910s–1930s auto boom years — produced thousands of craftsman bungalows, colonial revivals and Georgian brick homes in neighborhoods like Palmer Woods, Indian Village, Rosedale Park and the Grosse Pointes, all featuring original masonry chimneys now 80–110 years old. Michigan winters are severe: Detroit averages 33 inches of annual snow with January lows around 18°F. The Great Lakes' lake-effect snow adds another layer, producing heavy, wet snow that sits on chimney crowns and accelerates water infiltration.

Detroit's unique challenge is a polarized real estate market: pristine historic homes in Grosse Pointe and Birmingham alongside deeply neglected properties in neighborhoods undergoing gentrification. For renovation projects in Detroit proper, chimney systems often haven't been maintained in decades — sometimes requiring complete liner replacement, full masonry repair and crown rebuilds before a fireplace can be safely used. Michigan also has a large wood stove and insert market given its cold winters and rural character outside the metro, requiring specific liner sizing and annual maintenance.

When to book: Detroit's chimney season runs October through April. Book in August. Lake-effect snow events are common from November through January, and post-storm inspection is recommended after heavy accumulations on chimney crowns.
