Chimney Care Basics for California Homeowners

A few habits keep any chimney safe and add years to its life, wherever you live in California. Have the chimney inspected once a year, even if you rarely light a fire, because water damage, animal intrusion and liner problems happen regardless of use. Have it cleaned whenever creosote buildup reaches about one-eighth of an inch, which for regular wood burners usually means once per heating season. Burn only seasoned, dry hardwood, since wet or green wood burns cool and produces far more of the creosote that causes chimney fires. Keep a sound cap on the flue to block rain, animals and embers, and make sure the crown and flashing are sealed so water stays out of the masonry. Finally, install carbon monoxide detectors on every level of your home as a backstop, because a blocked or cracked flue can push that odorless gas back inside.
