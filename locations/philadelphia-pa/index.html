Why Philadelphia Residents Choose Us

Philadelphia has the largest stock of pre-1940 housing of any major US city — over 60% of homes predate World War II. Row homes, twins and Victorians throughout Fishtown, Manayunk and Queen Village have original clay tile chimneys, some over a century old. Philadelphia's mid-Atlantic freeze-thaw cycle is particularly destructive: temperatures fluctuate above and below freezing multiple times per week, cracking mortar joints and spalling brick faster than in consistently cold climates. Our technicians know Philadelphia's chimney systems — from South Philly row home flues to the multiple-flue stacks of Chestnut Hill Victorians.

Philadelphia's combination of humidity and freeze-thaw cycling makes water infiltration the dominant chimney problem. Efflorescence, mortar cracking and crown deterioration are widespread in the city's older housing. Annual inspection is essential — and a post-winter inspection in April catches freeze-thaw damage before summer rain worsens it significantly.

When to book: Book August–September before the heating season, or April–May after winter to catch freeze-thaw damage. Both windows fill quickly.
