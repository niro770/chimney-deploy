Chimney Service in Dallas: What Local Homeowners Need to Know

Dallas winters are generally mild — January lows around 35°F — but the region experiences dramatic cold events, including the 2021 winter storm that caused widespread damage to Texas homes and chimneys. Even in normal winters, Dallas homeowners use fireplaces from November through February and often neglect chimney maintenance assuming their mild climate means lower risk.

Dallas has extensive suburban housing from the 1970s–2000s in Plano, Frisco, McKinney and Allen, alongside older homes in Oak Cliff, Lake Highlands and East Dallas. Many Dallas homes have gas log fireplaces rather than wood-burning units — these still require annual inspection. Gas log systems vent through the original masonry flue, which must be properly lined and clear of obstruction.

Dallas chimney season is short — primarily November through February. Schedule in October to guarantee service before the season. After extreme cold events, inspection is critical to catch any freeze damage that occurred during the storm.
