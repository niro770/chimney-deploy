Why New York City residents choose ChimneyServices.net

New York City's chimney landscape is unlike any other US market. Brownstones in Brooklyn and Harlem, pre-war co-ops on the Upper West Side, row houses in Queens and attached townhouses across all five boroughs share one characteristic: original brick chimney systems from the 1880s–1930s that have typically been converted multiple times — from coal to oil to gas — often without proper relining at each conversion. Many Manhattan and Brooklyn brownstones have four to six fireplace flues per chimney stack, all requiring independent inspection. New York winters are serious: January lows average 26°F with wind making rooftop chimney work on brownstones and rowhouses genuinely challenging — this is work for experienced professionals.

NYC's dense housing creates chimney access challenges that require specialized equipment and experienced crews. Many brownstone and co-op buildings have had fireplace mantels and surrounds bricked up or sealed — what appears to be a decorative fireplace may actually be an active flue serving another floor's furnace or water heater. Proper documentation of each flue's use is essential before any chimney work. NYC co-op and condo boards also increasingly require annual chimney inspection documentation as part of building safety compliance.

When to book: NYC chimney season runs October through March. Book in August–September. Many NYC property managers schedule all building chimney inspections simultaneously in fall — we handle both individual homeowners and full building contracts.
