Why Providence residents choose ChimneyServices.net

Providence and Rhode Island represent some of the oldest residential chimney systems in the United States. College Hill's Federal-style and Greek Revival homes date to the late 1700s and early 1800s — among the oldest continuously occupied residential chimneys in America. Providence's proximity to Narragansett Bay means coastal salt air corrosion adds an additional degradation factor beyond the New England freeze-thaw cycle. Rhode Island averages 31 inches of annual snow with January lows around 20°F and a heating season that runs October through April. The compact scale of Rhode Island also means Providence-based technicians serve every corner of the state within a short drive.

College Hill's Federal and Georgian homes from the 1790s–1840s have chimney systems of extraordinary age — some approaching 200 years of service. These require the most careful and preservation-sensitive inspection and repair approaches of any residential chimneys in the country. Brown University's historic campus and the surrounding College Hill neighborhood represent a concentration of 18th–19th century masonry construction that requires ongoing professional chimney maintenance. Coastal Rhode Island communities — Newport, Narragansett, Westerly — add salt air corrosion to the maintenance equation.
