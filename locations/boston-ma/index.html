Why Boston residents choose ChimneyServices.net

Greater Boston has some of the oldest housing in the United States. Beacon Hill brownstones, Cambridge triple-deckers and Somerville Victorians built in the 1880s–1920s often have original chimney liners — some 100–130 years old — that have never been inspected or replaced. Many homes originally burned coal; when converted to gas in the mid-20th century, chimneys were frequently left unrelined — flues now oversized for modern appliances and potentially deteriorated. Boston's intense winters (43 inches of annual snow, January highs of 36°F) add significant stress through repeated freeze-thaw cycling.

Boston's heating season runs October through May — nearly eight months — meaning chimneys see more annual use here than in most US markets. Triple-deckers with shared chimney stacks serving multiple flues need independent inspection of each flue to verify they aren't cross-connected (a code violation and fire hazard). After major nor'easters, wind-driven rain often enters at chimney caps and crowns — post-storm inspection is worthwhile after significant winter weather.

When to book: Book August–September before the season starts. Post-storm inspection after major nor'easters is also recommended. Our technicians are familiar with access challenges in Boston's dense urban neighborhoods.
