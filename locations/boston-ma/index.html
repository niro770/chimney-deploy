Chimney Service in Boston: What Local Homeowners Need to Know

Boston averages 43 inches of snow per year — more than Chicago — with January average highs of just 36°F. New England's intense winters and significant temperature swings make chimney maintenance critical. Boston homeowners rely on fireplaces from October through April, and the heavy snowfall can drive water into chimney crowns and accelerate freeze-thaw deterioration.

Greater Boston has among the oldest housing stock in the country. Many Beacon Hill brownstones, Cambridge triple-deckers and Somerville Victorians were built in the 1880s–1920s and have never had their original chimney liners replaced. These homes often have multiple flues venting coal furnaces that were converted to gas — a situation that often requires relining to safely vent modern appliances.

Boston's chimney season is long — roughly October through May. Schedule cleaning in September to beat the rush. Post-storm inspection is also recommended after major nor'easters, as wind-driven rain can force water into flue systems and dislodge chimney caps.
