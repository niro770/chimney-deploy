Why Atlanta Residents Choose ChimneyServices.net

Atlanta has short, mild winters — January lows average 35°F and snow is rare. The fireplace season is typically December through February. Despite the mild climate, Atlanta chimneys are frequently found in poor condition: homeowners assume they don't need service, years pass between inspections, and moisture and animal intrusion cause significant damage in the meantime. Atlanta's warm, humid climate and abundant tree canopy make chimneys an attractive nesting site for birds, raccoons and squirrels — a problem unique to the mild Southeast.

Atlanta's housing ranges from 1920s–1940s bungalows in Candler Park, Grant Park and Inman Park to mid-century ranches in Decatur and new construction across North Atlanta suburbs. Many Atlanta homes have decorative fireplaces that have never been used — which doesn't mean they're safe or maintenance-free. Unused fireplaces still accumulate debris, animal nests and moisture damage that must be addressed before the fireplace can be safely used or, in some cases, before the home can be sold.

When to book: Atlanta's cleaning season peaks October–November before the short winter, and March after the season ends. Because the season is short, October is the booking deadline — quality technicians fill up by late October.
