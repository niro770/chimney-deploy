Why Chicago residents choose ChimneyServices.net

Chicago winters are severe: average January lows below 20°F with wind chills reaching -30°F or colder mean fireplaces run October through April — a six-month burn season that builds creosote faster than nearly any other US market. The relentless freeze-thaw cycle cracks mortar joints in ways invisible from outside the chimney. We know Chicago's stock: clay tile flue systems in pre-war two-flats and greystones, shared chimney stacks in courtyard buildings, and the draft dynamics of tall Chicago rowhouses. We serve Lincoln Park, Wicker Park, Bridgeport, Hyde Park, Andersonville and all of Chicagoland.

Over 40% of Chicago homes predate 1950. These properties typically have original clay tile liners — some 80–100 years old — that crack under Chicago's freeze-thaw cycle. When gas inserts were added without relining, the result is an undersized or deteriorated liner: a fire and CO hazard. Many Chicago homeowners discover for the first time during inspection that their fireplace has been unsafe for years. Don't wait — an inspection is the only way to know for certain.

When to book: Book in August or September. By mid-October, chimney services across Chicagoland are booked 4–6 weeks out as homeowners scramble before first frost.
