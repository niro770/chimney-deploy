Chimney Service in Seattle: What Local Homeowners Need to Know

Seattle's chimney challenges are different from cold-climate cities. The Pacific Northwest gets over 37 inches of rainfall annually with long stretches of overcast, damp weather from October through June. This persistent moisture is the primary enemy of Seattle chimneys — water infiltration through deteriorated crowns and caps is far more damaging here than freeze-thaw cycling. Dryer vent cleaning is also critically important in Seattle's damp climate.

Seattle's housing stock includes Craftsman bungalows from the 1910s–30s in Capitol Hill and the Central District, mid-century homes in Bellevue and Kirkland, and newer construction in South Lake Union and Ballard. Many Seattle homes have wood-burning fireplaces that owners use infrequently, creating a false sense that cleaning isn't needed — but even occasional use creates creosote, and moisture damage happens regardless of use.

Seattle's chimney work peaks in fall (September–October) before the rainy season intensifies, and in spring (April–May) for post-season inspection. Because Seattle rarely gets hard freezes, moisture damage rather than freeze-thaw is the primary concern.
