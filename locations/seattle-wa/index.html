Why Seattle residents choose ChimneyServices.net

Seattle's chimney challenges are distinct from cold-climate cities. The Pacific Northwest receives over 37 inches of rainfall annually with long stretches of overcast, damp weather from October through June. This persistent moisture — not extreme cold — is the primary enemy of Seattle chimneys. Water infiltration through deteriorated crowns, failed flashing and rusted dampers is far more common here than freeze-thaw cracking. Dryer vent cleaning is also critically important in Seattle's damp climate, where clothes take longer to dry and lint buildup is accelerated.

Seattle's housing stock includes Craftsman bungalows from the 1910s–30s in Capitol Hill and the Central District, mid-century homes in Bellevue and Kirkland, and newer construction throughout South Lake Union and Ballard. Many Seattle homeowners have wood-burning fireplaces they use infrequently — sometimes once or twice per season — and assume this means minimal maintenance. But even occasional use creates creosote, and moisture damage happens regardless of use frequency. An uninspected fireplace is an uninspected fire hazard.

When to book: Seattle's chimney work peaks in September–October before the rainy season intensifies, and in April–May for post-season inspection. Because Seattle rarely gets hard freezes, moisture management — not freeze-thaw — is the year-round concern.
