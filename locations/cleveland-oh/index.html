Why Cleveland Residents Choose Us

Cleveland's Great Lakes location creates one of the most chimney-intensive climates in the US. Lake Erie's lake-effect snow belt delivers 57 inches of annual snowfall to Cleveland — among the highest of any major city — with heavy, wet lake-effect events from November through January that sit on chimney crowns and accelerate water infiltration. January lows average 21°F with wind chills regularly below zero. Cleveland's housing stock skews heavily toward pre-war construction: craftsman bungalows in Tremont and Ohio City, colonial revivals in Shaker Heights and Bratenahl, and Italianate row houses near University Circle all have original chimney systems from the 1900s–1930s that are still in active use.

Northeast Ohio's industrial heritage means many Cleveland-area homes converted from coal heating multiple times — first to oil, then to natural gas — often without relining the original coal-scale flues at each conversion. These oversized, deteriorated flues are a hidden hazard in thousands of Cleveland homes. The lake-effect snow zone also means Cleveland chimneys see more annual precipitation on the crown than almost any other US market, accelerating crown and mortar deterioration significantly.

When to book: Cleveland's chimney season runs October through April. The lake-effect window extends from November through January with the heaviest events. Book in August–September. Post-storm inspection after major lake-effect events is strongly recommended.
