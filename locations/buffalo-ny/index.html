Why Buffalo residents choose ChimneyServices.net

Buffalo is arguably the most chimney-intensive major market in the United States. The combination of Lake Erie lake-effect snow (94 inches annually — among the highest of any US city), January lows of 17°F, a heating season that runs October through May, and some of the densest stock of pre-1940 masonry housing in the country creates a perfect storm of chimney maintenance demand. Buffalo's famous blizzards — including the legendary 1977 storm and the 2022 Christmas blizzard — regularly deposit several feet of snow on chimney crowns, and the lake-effect snow belt extends from Buffalo south through Hamburg and east through Cheektowaga. Every year that maintenance is deferred compounds the damage in a climate this extreme.

Buffalo's architectural heritage is extraordinary — the city's late 19th-century prosperity produced blocks of meticulously built brick and stone homes in Allentown, Elmwood Village and North Buffalo that are now among the most affordable historic housing in any major US city. These homes have original chimney systems from the 1880s–1910s — some 110–140 years old — that have survived Buffalo's extreme winters because they were built to extremely high standards. But no chimney survives indefinitely without professional maintenance, and Buffalo's climate is unforgiving of neglect. The lake-effect snow season extends from November through January with periodic events possible through March.

When to book: Buffalo's chimney season runs October through May — the longest of any major US market. Book in August. Post-blizzard inspection after major lake-effect events is strongly recommended. The weight of lake-effect snow accumulation on chimney crowns is significant even by Midwest standards.
