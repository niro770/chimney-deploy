Why Columbus residents choose ChimneyServices.net

Columbus sits at the intersection of Central Ohio's four distinct seasons: hot, humid summers and cold winters with 27 inches of annual snow and January lows around 23°F. The city's explosive growth — Columbus is now Ohio's largest city — has created a market that spans Victorian-era homes in German Village and Olde Towne East, mid-century colonials in Bexley and Upper Arlington, and brand-new construction in Dublin, Hilliard and New Albany. Each housing era has distinct chimney characteristics: Victorian homes have original masonry systems from the 1880s–1900s, mid-century homes often have unlined or improperly sized gas fireplace flues, and new construction has factory-built prefab systems requiring different maintenance protocols.

German Village's 19th-century brick row houses are among the most charming and chimney-intensive properties in Columbus. Many feature original fireplaces with clay tile liners that have been patched and repatched over a century of use. The university district's older rental stock — heavily converted Victorians and Foursquares near OSU — frequently has poorly maintained chimney systems that current tenants and owners inherit without knowing their condition.

When to book: Columbus's chimney season runs November through March. Book in September–October. Columbus's location south of the lake-effect belt means less snow than Cleveland but still significant freeze-thaw cycling that damages masonry year over year.
