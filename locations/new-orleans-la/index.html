Why New Orleans residents choose ChimneyServices.net

New Orleans is one of the most architecturally distinctive chimney markets in the United States. The city's Creole cottages, shotgun houses and Greek Revival antebellum homes in the Garden District, Uptown and Faubourg Marigny contain some of the oldest continuously occupied residential chimneys in the country — many dating to the 1840s–1860s. Louisiana's subtropical humidity — among the highest in the continental US — is the dominant chimney threat: persistent moisture infiltration through deteriorated mortar and crowns causes year-round damage without any freeze-thaw contribution. New Orleans averages only 0.1 inches of snow annually but receives 62 inches of rainfall — every drop of which is an opportunity for chimney moisture damage.

Post-Katrina New Orleans has seen massive renovation investment in its historic neighborhoods, but chimney systems are frequently overlooked in renovation budgets. Many Garden District and Uptown properties have had their interiors completely renovated while the original 1860s–1890s chimney systems remain unexamined. These antebellum chimneys — built for wood and then coal combustion — are now being asked to vent gas inserts, wood stoves and decorative gas log sets without the liner upgrades that safe operation requires. Hurricane exposure adds another layer: any Category 1 or stronger storm can damage chimney crowns and caps with wind-driven rain that penetrates at velocities that normal rain cannot.

When to book: New Orleans barely has a chimney season — December through February, and only on the coldest nights. But annual inspection is essential given the humidity exposure and hurricane season. Schedule in November before the brief cool period and before any year-end renovation work.
